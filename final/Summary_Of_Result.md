# Summary of Result

The development of **SkillSwap**, a knowledge exchange platform for peer-to-peer knowledge sharing, was carried out successfully with the rigorous use of the **Agile development model**. Through the iterative and incremental approach, the team successfully transformed the user requirements to a working minimum viable product (MVP) in two sprint phases.

The results have shown the effectiveness of the Agile framework in a multi-disciplinary academy environment. Sprint 1 brought the architectural foundation and essential authenticate techniques, while Sprint 2 focused on UI/UX improvement and efficacy implementation of a connection system based on constant feedback. Key artifacts, including a prioritized Trello backlog and high-fidelity Figma prototypes, ensured strict alignment between technical execution and project milestones.

Ultimately, the project met its goal of spanning faculty silos via a scaleable digital marketplace. The software was successfully used to integrate the backend with a react frontend module to a real-time bulletin board backend based on supabase, which proved that the Agile model was effective for a rapid adaptation and high-quality delivery approach for complex student-led software projects.
